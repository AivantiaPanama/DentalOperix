# DentalOperix 61.3-00 — Patient Domain Discovery Report

**Status:** COMPLETE / APPROVED
**Date:** 2026-06-23
**Owner:** Architecture / Governance
**Predecessors:** 61.2 Assistant / Front Desk Workspace CLOSED / CERTIFIED; 61.2-06A/B/C Appointment Domain Discovery, Foundation and Assistant Workflow CERTIFIED
**Next Phase:** 61.3-01 Patient Identity Foundation

---

## 1. Executive Summary

61.3-00 formalizes the Patient domain before implementation. The discovery confirms that DentalOperix must not treat Patient as a derived view of Lead, Appointment, or Calendar. Patient is a permanent identity record for a person known by the clinic.

The product direction is therefore refined from **Patient Management Workspace** to **Patient Identity Platform** as the foundation for future clinic operations.

```text
Patient = permanent identity
Lead = acquisition / commercial-follow-up lifecycle
Appointment = scheduled operational event
Treatment Episode = future clinical/operational case domain
Billing / Insurance = future benefits, coverage, invoicing and discount domain
```

61.3-01 must implement the Patient identity foundation before UI-heavy workspace implementation.

## 2. Certified Constraints Preserved

The certified Leads persistence architecture remains intact:

```text
Leads
  -> LeadPersistencePort
  -> LeadPersistenceProvider
  -> RelationalLeadPersistenceAdapter
  -> Supabase PostgreSQL
```

The rule **Leads = Source of Truth** remains valid, with the refined scope:

```text
Leads = Source of Truth for acquisition, marketing, commercial follow-up and lead lifecycle.
Patients = Source of Truth for person identity.
Appointments = Source of Truth for scheduled operational events.
```

No decision in 61.3-00 reopens 57.x, replaces Leads, introduces Dual Write, or changes certified persistence.

## 3. Approved Domain Decisions

### PD-001 — Patient Identity

Patient is an independent, permanent identity record.

```text
Patient exists independently of Lead, Appointment, Treatment or Attendance.
```

A Patient may originate from:

- Web booking
- Chat
- WhatsApp
- Phone call
- Walk-in reception
- Lead creation
- Appointment request
- Assistant/Admin/Doctor entry

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-002 — Patient Identity Immutability

The patient does not change; patient data changes.

Examples:

```text
Same person + new phone = same Patient
Same person + new email = same Patient
Same person + new address = same Patient
```

The system must update attributes and contact points, not replace the Patient identity.

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-003 — Patient Data Normalization

Variable patient data must be normalized.

Recommended foundational tables:

```text
patients
patient_phones
patient_emails
patient_addresses
patient_identifiers
```

Future/extension tables:

```text
patient_insurance_policies
patient_contacts_or_guardians
patient_consents
patient_audit_events
patient_merge_events
```

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-004 — Patient Lifecycle States

Approved Patient states:

```text
active
inactive
lost_contact
archived
```

Interpretation:

- `active`: active patient or active candidate for clinic services.
- `inactive`: known patient without recent activity but recoverable.
- `lost_contact`: contact attempts failed or patient stopped responding.
- `archived`: retained for administrative/history purposes, not operationally active.

Patients must not be deleted as a normal workflow. They change state.

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-005 — Creation Rules by Source

#### Web / Public Channel Minimum

```text
name
email
created_at automatic
```

Optional:

```text
phone
```

#### Clinic / Internal Channel Minimum

```text
first_name
last_name(s)
primary_phone
created_at automatic
status default
requires_invoice
CID or equivalent identifier when invoice is required
email optional
```

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-006 — Duplicate Resolution

The system must avoid duplicate Patient identities.

Initial resolution strategy:

1. Strong match by normalized name + email.
2. If same name but ambiguity exists, request a third differentiator such as second surname, CID, phone, or another identifier.
3. Do not automatically merge uncertain matches.
4. Manual merge is allowed only through authorized users.

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-007 — Manual Patient Merge

Patient merge is allowed but must be manual and controlled.

Minimum audit fields for future implementation:

```text
merged_from_patient_id
merged_into_patient_id
merged_by_user_id
merged_at
merge_reason
```

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-008 — Patient Creation from Lead / Appointment

A Patient may be created automatically when a Lead is generated or when an Appointment is requested/scheduled if no matching Patient exists.

This does not mean Lead is replaced by Patient.

```text
Lead = campaign/commercial lifecycle
Patient = identity
```

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-009 — Extended Lead Lifecycle

A Lead does not necessarily end when an appointment is scheduled.

The Lead lifecycle may span:

```text
captación
pre-cita
cita
seguimiento
tratamiento
cierre por doctor
cierre por abandono / ausencia / tiempo
```

This creates an Architecture Watch Item for a future Treatment Episode or Case domain.

Status: **APPROVED / WATCHED**

---

### PD-010 — Insurance and Retired Patient Boundary

Patient may register administrative indicators:

```text
retirement_status / is_retired
insurance_status / has_insurance
```

Insurance details may be captured at a basic level in future patient records, but coverage rules, discount calculations, billing rules, and plan conditions belong to a future Insurance / Benefits / Billing domain.

Status: **APPROVED**

---

### PD-011 — Reusable Domain Architecture

61.3 must follow reusable domain architecture principles.

```text
Domain
  -> Application Service
  -> Repository / Adapter
  -> Infrastructure
  -> UI
```

UI must not own business rules for identity resolution, duplicate detection, merge, insurance boundary, lifecycle, or audit.

Status: **APPROVED**

## 4. Proposed Patient Identity Foundation Scope for 61.3-01

61.3-01 must build foundation before workspace UI.

Recommended scope:

- Patient domain types and status model.
- Patient identity validation.
- Patient contact point contracts.
- Patient identifier contracts.
- Patient repository interface.
- Patient service layer.
- Identity resolution policy skeleton.
- Manual merge contract skeleton.
- Audit contract skeleton.
- SQL migration proposal for normalized patient tables.
- Tests for status, validation, identity resolution and repository mapping.

Not in scope for 61.3-01:

- Full patient workspace UI.
- Doctor ↔ Patient assignment.
- Clinical record / EHR.
- Treatment plans.
- Insurance coverage calculation.
- Billing automation.
- Multi-clinic tenancy implementation.
- Automated patient merge.

## 5. Architecture Watch Items

### AWI-61.3-001 — Future Treatment Episode Domain

Lead is currently absorbing commercial follow-up and some post-appointment/treatment lifecycle semantics. Evaluate whether a future Treatment Episode / Case domain should own treatment lifecycle.

### AWI-61.3-002 — Guardian / Responsible Party

Minor patients may require parent/guardian/responsible party contact, billing, and authorization support.

### AWI-61.3-003 — Consent & Marketing Preferences

Marketing and communication consent should be modeled before automated campaigns use patient/lead contact data extensively.

### AWI-61.3-004 — Multi-Clinic Patient Identity

Future product growth may require tenant-aware patient identity. Assess whether Patient is global or clinic-scoped.

### AWI-61.3-005 — Insurance & Benefits Domain

Retired status, insurance policy, coverage and discount rules must not be embedded directly into Patient identity business rules.

### AWI-61.3-006 — Patient Audit Trail

Changes to phone, email, address, CID, status, insurance flags and merge operations should become auditable.

### AWI-61.3-007 — Patient Identity Resolution

Duplicate detection, ambiguity handling and identity resolution must be centralized and reusable.

### AWI-61.3-008 — Patient Contact Point Management

A Patient may have multiple changing contact points without changing identity.

### AWI-61.3-009 — Marketing Consent Domain

Lead and Patient data used for campaigns must eventually respect explicit channel preferences and opt-out state.

### AWI-61.3-010 — Tenant Boundary Assessment

Assess tenant boundaries before expanding beyond one clinic or deploying multi-clinic models.
