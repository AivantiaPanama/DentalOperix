## 3. Domain Definition

A Patient is the permanent identity record for a person known by the clinic.

Patient is not:

- a Lead
- an Appointment
- a Calendar event
- a Treatment Episode
- an Insurance policy
- a Billing account
- a marketing lifecycle record

Patient exists independently of how the person first entered the system.

Approved principle:

```text
The patient does not change; patient attributes and contact points change.
```

---

## 4. Source-of-Truth Boundary

The certified source-of-truth model is:

```text
Leads = Source of Truth for acquisition, marketing and lead lifecycle.
Patients = Source of Truth for person identity.
Appointments = Source of Truth for scheduled operational events.
```

Therefore:

- Lead may reference or originate a Patient.
- Appointment may reference a Patient.
- Patient must not replace Lead.
- Patient must not replace Appointment.
- Lead must not own permanent person identity.
- Appointment must not own permanent person identity.

---

## 5. Patient Responsibilities

The Patient domain owns:

1. Permanent person identity.
2. Patient lifecycle state.
3. Normalized patient attributes.
4. Normalized contact points.
5. Identity identifiers.
6. Duplicate detection policy.
7. Manual merge policy and contracts.
8. Identity audit contract foundation.

---

## 6. Patient Non-Responsibilities

The Patient domain does not own:

1. Lead acquisition funnel.
2. Marketing campaign lifecycle.
3. Appointment scheduling workflow.
4. Calendar synchronization.
5. Gmail notification delivery.
6. Treatment plans.
7. Clinical EHR.
8. Billing automation.
9. Insurance coverage calculation.
10. Automated duplicate merging.
11. Doctor-to-patient permanent assignment.
12. Multi-clinic tenancy rules, unless separately authorized in a future phase.

---

## 7. Approved Identity Model

Patient identity is stable and permanent.

Mutable patient data includes:

- phones
- emails
- addresses
- identifiers
- administrative indicators
- lifecycle state

Identity must not be replaced because contact information changes.

Valid operations include:

```text
create Patient
update patient attributes
add contact point
update contact point
deactivate contact point
change lifecycle state
record identity audit event
manually merge duplicate patient records, if future-authorized
```

Invalid ordinary operations include:

```text
replace Patient because phone changed
replace Patient because email changed
merge automatically without human authorization
delete Patient as normal workflow
use Patient as a Lead replacement
use Appointment as identity source of truth
```
