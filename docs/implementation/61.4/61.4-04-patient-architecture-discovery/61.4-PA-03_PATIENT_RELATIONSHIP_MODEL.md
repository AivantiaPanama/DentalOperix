## 1. Purpose

Define the conceptual relationship model between Patient and the surrounding DentalOperix domains.

This document defines boundaries only. It does not authorize database relations, APIs, migrations, services, or UI changes.

---

## 2. Source-of-Truth Boundaries

| Domain       | Source of Truth For                    |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| Leads        | Acquisition, marketing, lead lifecycle |
| Patients     | Person identity                        |
| Appointments | Scheduled operational events           |

No relationship may invert or weaken these boundaries.

---

## 3. Patient to Lead Relationship

### Conceptual Relationship

A Lead may become associated with a Patient when evidence indicates that the lead represents or relates to a known person identity.

### Ownership Rules

Patient may know that a Lead is associated with it, but Patient does not own:

- lead source
- campaign
- lead status
- lead conversion lifecycle
- acquisition metadata

Lead remains the source of truth for lead lifecycle.

### Allowed Relationship Semantics

- proposed association
- confirmed association
- rejected association
- historical association

### Prohibited Semantics

- Patient replaces Lead
- Patient becomes lead lifecycle owner
- Lead data is dual-written into Patient as source-of-truth data

---

## 4. Patient to Appointment Relationship

### Conceptual Relationship

An Appointment may be associated with a Patient when the scheduled operational event is for that person.

### Ownership Rules

Patient may know that appointments exist, but Patient does not own:

- schedule state
- appointment status
- calendar operations
- provider availability
- appointment lifecycle

Appointment remains the source of truth for scheduled operational events.

### Allowed Relationship Semantics

- appointment linked to patient identity
- appointment identity confirmation pending
- appointment identity corrected through governed process

### Prohibited Semantics

- Appointment becomes identity owner
- Patient becomes scheduling lifecycle owner
- Appointment identity data silently overwrites Patient identity

---

## 5. Patient to Future Clinical Records Relationship

### Conceptual Relationship

Future clinical records may relate to Patient identity.

### Ownership Rules

Patient should own person identity, but future clinical records should own clinical facts, treatment notes, procedures, diagnoses, and clinical history.

### Governance Note

Clinical record architecture is outside the current 61.4 scope.

No clinical source-of-truth decision is authorized by this document.

---

## 6. Patient to Contact Points Relationship

### Conceptual Relationship

Contact points may support identity and communication, but they are not identity by themselves.

### Important Rule

Email, phone, and name are signals, not absolute identity proofs.

---

## 7. Relationship State Model

Candidate states for cross-domain associations:

| State        | Meaning                                                       |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| PROPOSED     | System or user suggests association.                          |
| CONFIRMED    | Authorized human or governed process confirms association.    |
| REJECTED     | Association is explicitly rejected.                           |
| UNDER_REVIEW | Additional review required.                                   |
| HISTORICAL   | Association remains for traceability but is no longer active. |

---

## 9. Discovery Conclusion

Patient relationships should be modeled as governed associations, not ownership transfers.

This preserves domain autonomy and prevents architecture drift.

Implementation remains NOT AUTHORIZED.
