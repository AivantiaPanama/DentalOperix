## 3. Patient Aggregate Candidate

### Aggregate Root

`Patient`

The Patient aggregate represents the durable identity of a person in DentalOperix.

It is not a lead, appointment, clinical note, marketing contact, or scheduling artifact.

---

## 4. Conceptual Responsibilities

The Patient aggregate owns:

- person identity
- identity attributes
- identity correction state
- patient contact identity context
- duplicate review state
- identity governance status
- audit-relevant identity changes

The Patient aggregate does not own:

- lead acquisition source
- marketing lifecycle
- appointment scheduling lifecycle
- calendar availability
- Gmail state
- clinical treatment records
- automated merge behavior

## 6. Candidate Invariants

The following invariants should be preserved in future design work:

1. A Patient represents a person identity, not a marketing lead.
2. A Patient must not become the source of truth for lead lifecycle.
3. A Patient must not become the source of truth for scheduled operational events.
4. Email alone is not sufficient identity proof.
5. Phone alone is not sufficient identity proof.
6. Name alone is not sufficient identity proof.
7. Duplicate detection must not imply merge.
8. Confirmed duplicate status must not imply automated consolidation.
9. Identity changes must be auditable.
10. Patient identity correction must not bypass RBAC.

## 9. Discovery Conclusion

The Patient aggregate should be treated as the architectural owner of person identity only.

It may reference Lead and Appointment contexts, but it must not absorb their responsibilities.

Implementation remains NOT AUTHORIZED.
