# 61.4-ID-01 IDENTITY MATRIX

Phase: 61.4  
Track: Identity Discovery  
Status: COMPLETE

## Objective

Define which attributes participate in Patient identity and the level of confidence each attribute provides.

## Core Principle

Patient identity must not depend on a single mutable or shareable attribute.

## Attribute Classification

| Attribute | Identity Strength | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| PatientId | Very High | Internal identifier after Patient creation. |
| Email | Medium | Can be shared across family members or caregivers. |
| Phone | Medium | Can be shared across households or family members. |
| Full Name | Low | High collision risk. |
| Date of Birth | Medium | Useful but may be missing or mistyped. |
| Full Name + Date of Birth | High | Stronger discriminator. |
| Full Name + Email | High | Useful candidate signal, not absolute proof. |
| Full Name + Phone | High | Useful candidate signal, not absolute proof. |
| Lead Origin | Very Low | Marketing context, not identity. |
| Appointment History | Low | Contextual, not identity ownership. |

## Strong Signals

- PatientId
- Full Name + Date of Birth
- Full Name + Date of Birth + Phone

## Medium Signals

- Email
- Phone
- Full Name + Email
- Full Name + Phone

## Weak Signals

- Name only
- Lead source
- Marketing campaign
- Isolated appointment

## Conclusion

No individual attribute, except an already-created PatientId, should be treated as absolute proof of person identity.
