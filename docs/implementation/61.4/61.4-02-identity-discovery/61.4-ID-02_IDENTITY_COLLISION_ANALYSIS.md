# 61.4-ID-02 IDENTITY COLLISION ANALYSIS

Phase: 61.4  
Track: Identity Discovery  
Status: COMPLETE

## Objective

Analyze scenarios where multiple records appear to represent the same person but may actually represent different people.

## Collision Types

### Shared Family Phone

A parent, spouse, child or caregiver may share the same phone number.

Decision: Phone must not be a unique identity key.

### Shared Email

A family or household email may represent multiple people.

Decision: Email must not be a unique identity key.

### Common Names

Names such as common first-name / last-name combinations collide frequently.

Decision: Name alone must never identify a Patient.

### Multiple Leads for the Same Person

Facebook, Google, WhatsApp or direct leads may all represent the same person.

Decision: Multiple Leads may relate to one Patient, but Leads remains Source of Truth for lead lifecycle.

### Appointment Before Patient Creation

An appointment may exist before full identity is formalized.

Decision: Appointment must not become owner of person identity.

### Minor Patients

A child patient may use a parent or guardian contact channel.

Decision: Contact channel and clinical/person identity are distinct concepts.

## Critical Findings

- Email does not equal person.
- Phone does not equal person.
- Name does not equal person.
- Multiple leads may correspond to the same person.
- Automatic merge is unsafe and remains prohibited.
