## 60.4 Runtime Persistence Alignment Note

60.4 remediates runtime drift discovered after the certified 57.x closure. Lead creation, lead listing, and derived read models must access Leads through `LeadPersistenceProvider` and the certified relational adapter by default. Google Sheets is restricted to explicitly approved rollback mode only and must not be used for dual write or as a parallel source of truth.

## Program Objective

Clinical Intelligence extends DentalOperix analytics from commercial and executive performance into patient and treatment execution analytics.

The program answers:

- How many patients have treatment plans?
- How many treatment plans are active or completed?
- How many treatment stages are completed?
- How much clinical outcome coverage exists?
- What data-quality gaps limit clinical interpretation?

## Architectural Position

```text
Clinical Read Models
  -> ClinicalReadAggregate
  -> ClinicalIntelligenceSnapshot
  -> Clinical Analytics API
  -> Executive / Operational Dashboards
```

Clinical Intelligence is read-only. It does not write clinical data, does not create a new master-data source, and does not alter the certified persistence architecture.

## Protected Components

The program must not modify:

- `BookingDialog`
- `processDentalLead`
- `/api/leads/create`
- Calendar integration
- Gmail integration
- `FloatingDentalAIChat`
- `Home`
- `siteServices.ts`

## Initial Scope

60.2 introduces:

- `ClinicalIntelligenceSnapshot`
- `createClinicalIntelligenceSnapshot()`
- `/api/analytics/clinical`
- `fetchClinicalIntelligence()`
- quality and governance limitations

## Out of Scope

- new clinical persistence tables
- clinical writes
- treatment plan mutation
- patient mutation
- source-of-truth changes
- revenue recognition
- billing ledger logic
- machine-learning diagnosis or treatment recommendation

## Governance Notes

Every 60.x output must identify itself as derived analytics. Clinical Intelligence v1 reports coverage and execution signals only; it does not make clinical decisions or modify patient records.

## 60.4-HF3 Clinic Email Delivery Guarantee

STATUS: IMPLEMENTED  
TYPE: Runtime Booking Notification Hotfix  
ARCHITECTURE CHANGE: NO  
SOURCE OF TRUTH CHANGE: NO  
DUAL WRITE: NO

60.4-HF3 addresses the remaining clinic email visibility gap after 60.4-HF2. Supabase lead persistence, Calendar delivery, and patient email delivery were confirmed. The unresolved issue was the clinic operational email not appearing when the clinic recipient resolved to the same account used as `GMAIL_SENDER`.

Implemented behavior:

```text
Patient email remains explicitly sent.
Clinic operational email remains explicitly sent.
Clinic self-notifications are tagged with notification audience metadata.
When clinic recipient equals Gmail sender and Gmail returns a message id, the system attempts to mark the message as INBOX and UNREAD.
Lead persistence is not rolled back if Gmail cannot mark a self-notification as inbox/unread.
```

Reference: `docs/architecture/60.4-HF3_CLINIC_EMAIL_DELIVERY_GUARANTEE.md`.

## 60.4-HF4 Unified Patient Confirmation Email

Status: IMPLEMENTED / READY FOR USER VALIDATION

60.4-HF4 consolidates patient booking notifications into a single patient-facing email. After 60.4-HF2/HF3 validation, the patient was receiving both a confirmation email and a separate Calendar/ICS notification. HF4 suppresses duplicate Calendar API attendee email delivery and attaches `invite.ics` to the patient confirmation email.

Certified architecture remains unchanged: Leads remain Source of Truth through `LeadPersistenceProvider` and `RelationalLeadPersistenceAdapter` to Supabase PostgreSQL. Calendar and Gmail remain downstream notification/integration services and do not become sources of truth.

Reference: `docs/architecture/60.4-HF4_UNIFIED_PATIENT_CONFIRMATION_EMAIL.md`.
