## Purpose

60.4-HF2 closes the gap found after restoring relational lead persistence: the booking flow could persist the lead in Supabase and create a clinic calendar event, but notification behavior was not explicit enough for non-clinic patient emails.

Final expected behavior for public booking confirmation:

```text
BookingDialog
  -> createDentalAppointment
  -> processDentalLead
  -> LeadPersistenceProvider
  -> RelationalLeadPersistenceAdapter
  -> Supabase PostgreSQL
  -> Google Calendar clinic event
  -> Patient attendee notification
  -> Clinic notification email
  -> Patient confirmation email
```

## Functional Contract

When a patient confirms a booking:

1. The lead is saved in Supabase through the certified persistence adapter.
2. The clinic calendar receives the event.
3. The patient email is added as an attendee of the Google Calendar event.
4. The clinic notification email is also added as an attendee unless it is duplicated.
5. Google Calendar uses attendee update delivery (`sendUpdates: "all"`).
6. Gmail sends a confirmation email to the patient.
7. Gmail sends an operational notification email to the clinic.
8. If email delivery fails, the lead remains saved and the calendar result remains preserved.
9. The booking response reports patient and clinic email delivery separately.

## Governance Compatibility

Certified source of truth remains unchanged:

```text
Leads = Source of Truth
```

Certified persistence path remains unchanged:

```text
Leads
  -> LeadPersistencePort
  -> LeadPersistenceProvider
  -> RelationalLeadPersistenceAdapter
  -> Supabase PostgreSQL
```

This hotfix does not introduce dual write. Calendar and Gmail are downstream notifications only; they are not a source of truth.

## Configuration Contract

The runtime must load Google and relational variables from the active environment file, for example `.env.local` in development.

Required/expected variables:

```env
LEADS_PERSISTENCE_MODE=relational-db
RELATIONAL_CUTOVER_APPROVED=true
RELATIONAL_RUNTIME_ACTIVATION_APPROVED=true
DATABASE_URL=...
GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID=...
GOOGLE_CLIENT_SECRET=...
GOOGLE_REDIRECT_URI=...
GOOGLE_REFRESH_TOKEN=...
GOOGLE_CALENDAR_ID=...
GOOGLE_CALENDAR_TIMEZONE=America/Panama
GMAIL_SENDER=clinic@example.com
CLINIC_NOTIFICATION_EMAIL=clinic@example.com
```

`CLINIC_NOTIFICATION_EMAIL` is optional and defaults to `GMAIL_SENDER` when omitted.

## Known Limitations

- Google Calendar can invite non-Google emails, but non-Google calendar auto-accept/auto-sync depends on the recipient provider. HF2 guarantees notification attempt and Gmail confirmation, not third-party calendar behavior outside Google.
- Gmail deliverability to external addresses can still be affected by sender reputation, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, spam filters, OAuth scopes, or workspace restrictions.
