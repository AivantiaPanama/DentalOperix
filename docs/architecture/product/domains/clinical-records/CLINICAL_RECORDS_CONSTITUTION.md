# Clinical Records Constitution

**Domain:** Clinical Records  
**Product:** DentalOperix  
**Status:** Active  
**Baseline:** DENTALOPERIX_BASELINE_71_5_RELEASE_CANDIDATE

## Preamble

Clinical Records exists to preserve the clinical history of people with respect for their dignity, privacy and wellbeing.

Behind every clinical record there is a person, a history and a relationship of trust between the patient and the professionals involved in their care.

For that reason, the clinical and personal information of a patient must be honored, respected and protected throughout its entire lifecycle.

Every functional, architectural, technical and operational decision related to this domain must be guided by human, medical, ethical and legal principles, preserving integrity, confidentiality, traceability and continuity of the clinical history.

Technology is the means to fulfill this mission; it is never the purpose.

## Identity statement

Clinical Records is the domain responsible for preserving longitudinal clinical information generated during patient care, ensuring integrity, traceability and controlled evolution without assuming responsibility for identity, scheduling or acquisition workflows.

## Mission

To protect the clinical memory of the patient through a clear, traceable and domain-owned clinical record.

## Responsibilities

Clinical Records owns:

- Clinical Notes.
- Clinical narrative information.
- Clinical history views.
- Future clinical assessments.
- Future diagnoses.
- Future treatment planning.
- Future dental charts.
- Future clinical attachments and prescriptions, if formally authorized.

## Non-responsibilities

Clinical Records does not own:

- Patient identity.
- Lead lifecycle.
- Appointment scheduling.
- User authentication.
- RBAC definition.
- Marketing workflows.

## Constitutional principles

### Principle I — Dignity of clinical information

Clinical and personal information must be honored, respected and protected because it represents part of the patient's health history and dignity.

### Principle II — Longitudinal continuity

A Clinical Record represents longitudinal clinical history and must evolve without losing continuity.

### Principle III — Clinical integrity

Clinical information must not be modified in a way that compromises traceability, accountability or historical meaning.

### Principle IV — Domain separation

Clinical Records must not assume responsibilities belonging to Leads, Patients or Appointments.

### Principle V — Controlled evolution

New clinical capabilities must be introduced through certified Work Packages and must preserve the certified architecture.

### Principle VI — Technology as means

Implementation choices must serve the domain mission and must not override human, clinical or legal responsibility.

## Bounded context

Clinical Records is the Clinical Information Domain of DentalOperix.

It references patient identity and may reference appointment context, but it owns only clinical information.

## Aggregate root

The conceptual aggregate root is:

- `ClinicalRecord`

It represents the longitudinal clinical record of one patient.

## First entity

The first functional entity introduced by WP-02 is:

- `ClinicalNote`

A Clinical Note is an individual clinical record entry generated by an authorized healthcare professional.

## Sources of Truth

Clinical Records is the Source of Truth for clinical information.

It is not the Source of Truth for:

- Leads.
- Patient identity.
- Appointment scheduling.

## Domain evolution

Clinical Records evolves through capability-driven Work Packages.

Current roadmap:

1. WP-01 Clinical Record Foundation — Certified.
2. WP-02 Clinical Notes Foundation — Active.
3. WP-03 Clinical Assessment — Candidate.
4. WP-04 Treatment Planning — Candidate.
5. WP-05 Dental Chart — Candidate.

## Governance rules

- Certified architectural decisions are not reopened without formal evidence.
- Clinical Records must not introduce Dual Write.
- Clinical Records must not trigger Persistence Re-Architecture.
- Protected components must not be modified by this domain without explicit authorization.
- Any expansion of clinical ownership must be documented and certified.
